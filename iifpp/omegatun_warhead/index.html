Omegaton Warhead

The Omegaton Warhead is a still-functional modified warhead derived from a dismantled [REDACTED] ICBM rocket. The site managed to obtain this asset through private nuclear weapon negotiations during the Second World War under the false name of ////////////////// for ////////. While the rest of the original delivery system was discarded on ////, the warhead itself was heavily modified around late 1898 to be remote-detonatable.

The Omegaton Warhead was originally installed by order of past site director Yohn Macjames as an emergency last-resort tool. It is designated for use in the event of a // scenario or the activation of a Level 5 Protocol Davidtun, with the intended goal to protect, destroy, and/or conceal any classified information in or about the facility.

The detonation console originally ran on an old command prompt system, later replaced with Raygan OS in the early 1960s. Recent intelligence has discovered major security flaws in this operating system: Since the computer uses math to determine if the user's access keycard has the required authority level of 5, it will accept negative numbers and null keycard serials. This allows the creation of fake negative authority-level cards such as "-1" or "-2" that the system reads as Level 5+.

While most keycard readers within the facility have been upgraded with modern safety features to prevent this exploit, modifying the Raygan OS core requires specialized technicians. These individuals are difficult to acquire without exposing them to the classified nature of the detonation console, and their termination would require excessive paperwork to cover up such an "INCIDENT".

Some outside sources report that an organization known as The Oil Merials is producing similar fake keycards, necessitating increased vigilance. The Omegaton Warhead is currently housed and stored within the Detention Sector.
