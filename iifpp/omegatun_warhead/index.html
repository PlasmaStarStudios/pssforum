Omegaton Warhead

the omegatun warhead is a still-functional modified warhead from the dismantled [REDACTED] ICBM rocket, which the site managed to obtain through private nuclear weapon negotiations during the second world war with ////////////////// under the fake name of ////////. the rest of the nuclear weapon was discarded on ////, and the warhead was modified around late 1898 to be remote-detonatable.

the omegatun warhead was originally installed by order of past site director Yohn Macjames as an emergency last-resort tool in the case of a class // scenario or the activation of a level 5 protocol davidtun, with the intended goal to protect, destroy, and/or conceal any classified information in or about the facility.

the detonation console originally ran on an old command prompt, later replaced with raygan os in the early 1960s, which has recently been discovered to have major security flaws. since the computer uses math to determine if the user's access keycard has the required authority level of 5, it will accept negative numbers and null keycard serials, which allows the creation of fake negative authority-level cards such as "-1", "-2", etc., that would be read as level 5+.

most keycard readers within the facility have modern safety features to prevent this, but modifying raygan os requires special technicians, who are difficult to acquire without exposing them to the classified nature of the detonation console. their termination would require too much paperwork to properly cover up such an "INCIDENT". some outside sources report that an organization by the name of the oil merials is producing similar fake keycards, so keep an eye out!. the omegatun warhead is currently housed and stored in the detention sector
